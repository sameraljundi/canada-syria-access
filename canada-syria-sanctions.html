Sanctions landscape

Canada eased broad economic prohibitions in February 2026 while keeping targeted sanctions in place.

Key milestones

Mar 2025: Canada issued a 6-month general permit for humanitarian, stabilization, and democratization-related transactions, including through seven Syrian banks.
Feb 2026: Canada removed 24 entities and one individual from the Syria Regulations to ease barriers to economic activity.
Dec 2025: Canada stated 56 Syrian entities and 225 Syrian individuals remained under sanctions.

What users need to know

  • Lawful does not always mean bankable.
  • Targeted listings and broader AML/CTF controls still matter.
  • Documentation, counterpart screening, and jurisdiction checks remain essential.
  • Use official sources before moving funds or entering contracts.

Primary references: Global Affairs Canada, Canada Gazette, Justice Laws, and World Bank Syria data.

About & methodology

This site is designed around official sources and practical decision-making, not generic commentary.

Core sources include Canada’s Syria regulations and updates, Global Affairs guidance, Canada Gazette amendments, World Bank Syria data, and relevant law-firm analysis for cross-jurisdiction comparison. The site should be updated whenever official measures change.

Brand direction: inspired by Syrian visual identity cues, but kept clean and policy-oriented for a Canadian audience.
