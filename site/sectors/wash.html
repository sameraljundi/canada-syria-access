Sectors · WASH

WASH / water infrastructure services

A coherent reconstruction-services lane under Canadian SEMA easing — demand class evidenced in World Bank infrastructure scope, with fewer Syria-specific Canadian deal precedents than power. Medium confidence. Not project or bank clearance.

Last reviewed: 22 July 2026 · Ranked #7 in contribution research

Disclaimer: Informational research only. This page does not itemize a Canadian project pipeline, clear counterparties, or guarantee financing.

Canada first

  • Goods and investment sectoral prohibitions were largely repealed in February 2026; targeted Schedule 1 listings and residual controls still bind. GAC Syria; SOR/2011-114
  • Screen every implementing partner, contractor, and payment intermediary. Delistings in recovery-critical sectors do not clear ownership chains. GAC backgrounder
  • Project finance and correspondent banking friction remain material even when Canadian legal room expands (operational analysis). See GAC guidance and Tools.

Demand & capability

Demand class (World Bank): Reconstruction best estimate US$216B (range $140–345B), of which US$82B infrastructure — infrastructure rebuild scope discussed alongside power, transport, telecom, and WASH — World Bank, 21 October 2025. Treat this as a demand category, not an itemized Canadian tender list. Assessment PDFs are not hosted on this site.

Canadian capability signal (weak / not Syria-specific): Canada exported $20.2B environmental and clean technology products in 2024 — StatCan The Daily, 17 February 2026.

Canadian contribution mode: water and WASH infrastructure services, engineering, and equipment supply where listings and payment rails allow — secondary to power-system services in contribution ranking.
