Started ≠ de-risked. A signed concession or operating port does not clear Schedule 1 listings, AML/CFT duties, bank institutional policy, or foreign regimes (US OFAC, EU, UK). See AML / CFT / KYC and GAC Syria.

Method

Five angles plus a skeptic pass. Every surviving finding required a URL and date; ceremony-only or single-sourced PR claims were demoted or dropped.

Stage stacks

Read the stage before the dollar headline.

Physical / ops

DP World Tartus — ops from Nov 2025; first mobile harbour crane CapEx Jul 2026. CMA CGM Latakia — long-running terminal ops, amended concession, AD Ports 20% equity, dry-port expansion.

Award / concession

SilkLink (stc / Tadawul) and Zain licence are awards — build/launch not yet verified as complete. UCC ~5 GW power and Damascus airport reached final concession language; plants/airport rebuild are pre-ops or early-works claimed.

Live tender

M5 / M45 highway packages (SANA, 14 Jul 2026) are open domestic and international tenders — not construction awards.

MoU / studies

Haditha–Baniyas and Chevron offshore Block 1 are real multi-source MoUs/studies — not FID or flowing crude / drilling.

Dropped or demoted

Claims that failed the skeptic pass (compact).

  • August 2025 “$14bn / 12 mega-projects finalized” — MoU ceremony, not financed build-out; UBAKO “Damascus Towers” delivery claim dropped.
  • TAV inside the $7bn energy consortium / single “$11bn energy deal” — TAV is airport track; $11bn is media bundling.
  • Starlink as a started commercial deal — unlicensed / authorization required.
  • World Bank US$216bn reconstruction estimate — cost frame, not a deal (WB, 21 Oct 2025.

Sector context for Canadian readers

Short paraphrase from State-funded Investor Guides — market friction colour only, not evidence that a mega-deal started. See References — Secondary US.

Banking & payments

Correspondent access, FX, and documentation packs often block activity even when Canadian sectoral rules ease. Port and concession cashflows still need a processable rail. Banking brief

Energy & power

Rehabilitation and generation themes attract interest; counterparties, beneficial owners, and goods classification remain first-order — mega-MoUs do not remove Schedule 1 risk. Energy brief

Telecom & technology

Connectivity demand is real; national fibre/MNO awards are Gulf-led. Canadian niche framing stays vendor / services, not backbone concession ambition. Telecom brief

Real estate & construction

Housing and reconstruction demand sit beside land governance and documentation risk — Marota-style projects illustrate started ≠ de-risked. Real estate brief
