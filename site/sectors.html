Economic access & sectors

Where Canadian-facing activity may emerge

Sector framing for rebuilding-related interest — always subordinate to sanctions screening and bankability. Deep sector briefs ship in the next sprint; this overview launches with clear caveats.

Research note: Opportunity framing is not a green light. February 2026 Canadian amendments repealed broad sectoral prohibitions while retaining targeted listings (GAC Syria sanctions). Listed-person risk, AML/CTF controls, and foreign-law exposure remain operational constraints (analysis, not statute).

Banking & payments

Payment rails, remittances, and compliance tooling sit at the centre of “lawful but not bankable” friction. Start with list screening and documentation packs. Guidance: GAC sanctions guidance.

Energy & infrastructure

Power, fuel logistics, and grid repair attract rebuilding interest. Confirm counterparties against remaining Schedule 1 listings and any controlled-goods issues. See Sanctions landscape.

NGO / early recovery path

Humanitarian and early-recovery programmes still need donor-safe workflows even after Canadian legal room expanded. Align with official humanitarian sanctions guidance where applicable.
